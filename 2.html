⚡ Topic 1: Electric Current & Drift Velocity
1.1 — Electric Current, Drift Velocity & Ohm's Law 🔴 VERY HIGH
I = nAev_d   |   v_d = eEτ/m   |   I = dQ/dt
📌 Current (I) = Rate of flow of charge = dQ/dt (unit: Ampere)
📌 Drift velocity (v_d) = Average velocity of electrons due to electric field
✅ v_d = eEτ/m  →  where τ = relaxation time, m = mass of electron
📌 Current equation: I = nAev_d  → n = free electrons per m³
Drift velocity is VERY SMALL (~10⁻³ m/s) but current flows instantly because E field propagates at speed of light.
Drift velocity is directly proportional to: Applied Voltage (V) ✅ | NOT temperature, NOT length alone

📋 PDF 3 — Q.10 PDF 3

Drift velocity of electrons is directly proportional to:
(1) Temperature
(2) Voltage applied ✅
(3) Length of the conductor
(4) Area of cross section
v_d = eEτ/m = e(V/L)τ/m
v_d ∝ V (voltage) at constant L, τ, m
✅ v_d directly ∝ Voltage applied

Conductors develop electric currents in them:
(1) On applying electric field ✅
(2) On placing in magnetic field
(3) On applying gravitational field only
(4) On applying magnetic + gravitational field
Electric current needs free electrons to move with NET velocity.
Free electrons move randomly without field → no net current.
Apply Electric Field → electrons get drift velocity → Current flows!

🔵 Topic 2: Resistance & Resistivity
2.1 — Ohm's Law, Resistance & Resistivity 🔴 VERY HIGH
R = ρL/A  |  ρ = m/ne²τ  |  V = IR
Resistance (R)

R = ρL/A
Depends on: Material, Length, Area, Temperature
Unit: Ohm (Ω)

Resistivity (ρ)

ρ = m/ne²τ
Depends on: Material + Temperature ONLY
NOT on dimensions!

🔴 Resistivity does NOT depend on length or area — only on material & temperature!
✅ Resistance depends on material, length, area AND temperature
ρ = mL / (ne²Aτ)  →  R = mL / (ne²Aτ) = ρL/A

📊 Resistivity Order (Increasing) — CUET Favourite!

MaterialResistivity (Ωm)Type
Silver (Ag)1.6×10⁻⁸Least → Best conductor
Copper (Cu)1.7×10⁻⁸Good conductor
Aluminium (Al)2.6×10⁻⁸Good conductor
Platinum (Pt)10.6×10⁻⁸Higher resistivity
Increasing resistivity order: Silver → Copper → Aluminium → Platinum (Remember: SCAP)

🟢 Topic 3: Electron Path in Electric Field
3.1 — Path of Electrons Between Collisions 🟡 MEDIUM
Without Electric Field (E=0)

Electrons move randomly
Between collisions → Straight line
(No force acting)

With Electric Field (E≠0)

Electrons accelerate
Between collisions → Curved path
(Like projectile motion)

No field → Straight line paths | With field → Curved paths (parabolic between collisions)

🟠 Topic 4: Electrical Power & Thermal Energy
4.1 — Power, Thermal Energy & Bulb Problems 🔴 VERY HIGH
P = VI = I²R = V²/R  |  H = I²Rt  |  W = Pt
📌 Joule's Law: H = I²Rt (heat produced in resistance R)
✅ For bulb rated P₀ at V₀: R_bulb = V₀²/P₀
⚡ Actual power at voltage V: P = V²/R = V² × P₀/V₀²
When bulb is connected to different voltage, use P = V²/R where R is fixed resistance of bulb!

🔵 Topic 6: EMF, Internal Resistance & Terminal Voltage
6.1 — Battery, EMF & Terminal Voltage 🔴 VERY HIGH
V_terminal = E - Ir  |  I = E/(R+r)
📌 E = EMF (open circuit voltage)
📌 r = internal resistance of battery
✅ Terminal voltage V = E - Ir (during discharge)
🔴 V = E + Ir during charging
Terminal voltage is ALWAYS less than EMF during discharge (due to voltage drop across internal resistance).

🟤 Topic 7: Galvanometer — Ammeter & Voltmeter Conversion
7.1 — Galvanometer Conversions (Very Important!) 🔴 VERY HIGH
🔴 To Ammeter

Connect LOW resistance (shunt S) in PARALLEL
S = I_gG/(I-I_g)
R_ammeter = GS/(G+S)

🔵 To Voltmeter

Connect HIGH resistance R in SERIES
R = V/I_g - G
R_voltmeter = G+R (very high)

Ammeter → PARALLEL (shunt) | Voltmeter → SERIES (multiplier) — Remember: A-P, V-S

8.1 — Wire Coil Problems & Wheatstone Bridge 🟡 MEDIUM-HIGH

📋 PDF 1 — Q.148 — Wire Coil PDF 1

A wire of resistance 4Ω is used to make a coil of radius 7cm. Wire diameter = 1.4mm, resistivity = 2×10⁻⁷ Ωm. Number of turns in the coil:
(1) 70 ✅
(2) 40
(3) 140
(4) 20
r_wire = 0.7mm = 7×10⁻⁴m → A = π(7×10⁻⁴)² = π×49×10⁻⁸
R = ρL/A → L = RA/ρ = 4×π×49×10⁻⁸/(2×10⁻⁷)
L = 4×154×10⁻⁸/2×10⁻⁷ = 4×154/20 = 30.8 m
Circumference per turn = 2πr = 2×π×0.07 = 0.44m
N = L/circumference = 30.8/0.44 ≈ 70 turns

📋 PDF 2 — Q.682 — Wheatstone Bridge PDF 2

The Wheatstone bridge null point condition is given by:
(1) R₁/R₂ = R₃/R₄ ✅
(2) R₁+R₂ = R₃+R₄
(3) R₁-R₂ = R₂-R₄
(4) R₁×R₂ = R₃×R₄
At null point: No current through galvanometer (middle arm)
Balance condition: R₁/R₂ = R₃/R₄
This means: R₁R₄ = R₂R₃ (cross multiply)
