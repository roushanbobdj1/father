Topic 1 — Types of Semiconductors & Band Gap

n-type Semiconductor

• Doped with Pentavalent atoms (P, As, Sb)
• Majority carriers: Electrons
• Minority carriers: Holes
• Extra electron (5th) is free
• Neutral overall (not charged!)

p-type Semiconductor

• Doped with Trivalent atoms (B, Al, In)
• Majority carriers: Holes
• Minority carriers: Electrons
• One bond incomplete (hole)
• Neutral overall!

Band gap for semiconductors: E_g < 3 eV (NOT > 3 eV — that's for insulators!). Si = 1.1eV, Ge = 0.67eV.
✅ As temperature increases → resistivity of semiconductor DECREASES (more carriers generated)
🔴 As temperature increases → resistivity of METALS increases (more collisions)

Topic 2 — p-n Junction Diode & Biasing

PDF1 Q181,182 PDF2 Q658 PDF3 Q30
Forward Bias: V_p > V_n (p side at higher potential)
Reverse Bias: V_n > V_p (n side at higher potential)
✅ Forward Bias

• p-side connected to +ve terminal
• Depletion layer DECREASES
• Potential barrier DECREASES
• Junction resistance DECREASES
• Current FLOWS (ON state)

❌ Reverse Bias

• n-side connected to +ve terminal
• Depletion layer INCREASES
• Potential barrier INCREASES
• Junction resistance INCREASES
• No current (OFF state)

Key trick for forward biased p-n junctions: Higher potential on p-side. If V_anode > V_cathode → Forward biased. Diode acts as switch: ON in forward, OFF in reverse bias.

Topic 5 — Maxwell's Equations & Displacement Current

PDF1 Q165 PDF3 Q17
I_d = ε₀ × dΦ_E/dt  (Displacement current)
Displacement current = ε₀ × rate of change of electric flux
c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3×10⁸ m/s  (Speed of EM waves)
📌 Maxwell introduced displacement current to fix Ampere's law (current through capacitor gap)
📌 Displacement current = ε₀ × dΦ_E/dt (due to CHANGING ELECTRIC FIELD)
✅ NOT due to flow of charges, NOT due to gravitational field
⭐ EM waves are produced by ACCELERATING CHARGES

I_d = ε₀ × dΦ_E/dt → due to changing ELECTRIC flux (not magnetic)
It's NOT due to physical charge flow — it's a fictitious current
Option 4 is wrong: I_d = ε₀ × d(E·A)/dt = ε₀×dΦ_E/dt (electric flux, NOT magnetic)

Topic 7 — Properties & Equations of EM Waves

PDF1 Q167 PDF2 Q670 PDF3 Q19
E = E₀ sin(kx - ωt)  |  B = B₀ sin(kx - ωt)
E₀/B₀ = c = ω/k  (Speed of EM wave)
c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3×10⁸ m/s  |  λ = 2π/k  |  f = ω/2π
