1 — Faraday's Laws & Lenz's Law

PDF1 Q158,164PDF3 Q46-50
ε = -N × dΦ/dt   (Faraday's Law — for N turns)
Φ = B·A·cosθ    (Magnetic flux)
Lenz's Law: Induced current OPPOSES the change in flux
Faraday's 1st Law

Flux changes → emf induced
No flux change → No emf

Faraday's 2nd Law

ε = -N×dΦ/dt
More N → more emf

🔴 Lenz's Law: Induced current OPPOSES the change → negative sign in Faraday's law
📌 "emf = total change of flux" is WRONG. Correct: emf = RATE of change (dΦ/dt)
✅ Faster magnet movement → LARGER emf (NOT smaller)
⚡ Bar magnet stationary → NO flux change → NO induced current
Most common CUET trap — emf = rate of flux change, NOT total flux change!

3 — AC Values: Peak, RMS & Average

PDF1 Q163
V = V₀ sin(ωt)             (AC voltage equation)
V_rms = V₀/√2 = 0.707 V₀   (RMS = root mean square)
Average (full cycle) = 0      (sine wave symmetry)
Average (half cycle) = 2V₀/π  (0.637 V₀)
Rated values are always RMS! 220V AC means V_rms=220V. Peak = 220√2 ≈ 311V
ValueFormulaFor 220V AC
Peak (V₀)√2 × V_rms311 V
RMSV₀/√2220 V (rated)
Avg (full cycle)00 V
Avg (half cycle)2V₀/π198 V

4 — Reactance, Impedance & Phase Angles

PDF2 Q639,668PDF3 Q37
X_L = ωL = 2πfL       (Inductive reactance)
X_C = 1/ωC = 1/2πfC   (Capacitive reactance)
Z = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²)  (Impedance)
tan φ = (X_L - X_C)/R    (Phase angle)
ElementPhase RelationPower
Resistor RV and I in phase (φ=0)P = I²R ≠ 0
Inductor LV leads I by 90°P = 0 (wattless)
Capacitor CI leads V by 90°P = 0 (wattless)
ELI the ICE man: E(voltage) leads I in L (Inductor). I leads E in C (Capacitor)

✅ Z = 5 Ω

5 — LCR Series Resonance

PDF1 Q161PDF2 Q667,668,669
ω₀ = 1/√(LC)      (Resonant angular frequency)
f₀ = 1/2π√(LC)   (Resonant frequency in Hz)
At resonance: X_L = X_C → Z = R (minimum) → I = maximum
Power factor cos φ = 1 at resonance
AT Resonance

X_L = X_C
Z = R (minimum!)
I = E/R (maximum!)
cos φ = 1
φ = 0° (V & I in phase)

OFF Resonance

f < f₀: X_C > X_L (capacitive)
f > f₀: X_L > X_C (inductive)
Z > R → I < maximum
cos φ < 1

⭐ At resonance: Z minimum, I maximum, Power factor = 1 (maximum power)
🔴 Voltage across L or C can be MUCH larger than supply voltage! (Q-factor effect)
📋 PDF 2 — Q.669 PDF2 Power Factor at Resonance
For LCR series circuit at resonance, power factor will be:
1. Zero
2. 1.0 ✅
3. 0.2
4. 0.5
At resonance: X_L = X_C → net reactance = 0 → Z = R
cos φ = R/Z = R/R = 1. Phase angle φ = 0°
✅ Power factor = 1 at resonance

6 — Power in AC & Transformer

P = V_rms × I_rms × cos φ  (Average power)
P = I²_rms × R           (Only R consumes power)
Transformer: V_s/V_p = N_s/N_p = I_p/I_s
🔴 Pure L or C: cos φ = 0 → Power = 0 (wattless current)
✅ Step-up: N_s > N_p → V_s > V_p but I_s < I_p
📌 Step-down: N_s < N_p → V_s < V_p but I_s > I_p
🎯 Practice Questions

A) 200 W ✅
B) 400 W
C) 346 W
D) 100 W
P = 200×2×cos60° = 400×0.5 = 200 W
P10. Primary: 100 turns, 220V. Secondary: 500 turns. Output voltage?
A) 1100 V ✅

V_s = V_p × (N_s/N_p) = 220 × 5 = 1100 V (step-up)
