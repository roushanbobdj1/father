🔥 5-Step RC Solving Strategy

1
Questions Pehle Padho (30 sec): Pehle saare 4–5 questions padho taaki passage mein answer dhundna easy ho. Focus points pata chal jayenge.
2
Passage Speed-Read Karo (2 min): Har paragraph ka main idea note karo mentally. First & last lines sabse important hoti hain.
3
Answer Location Technique: Question mein keyword dekho → passage mein woh keyword ya uska synonyms dhundo → answer wahan hoga.
4
Elimination Method: 4 options mein se clearly galat 2 hatao → baaki 2 mein passage ke evidence ke hisaab se choose karo.
5
Inference Questions: "Author ka purpose kya tha?" → Passage ke TONE se samjho: informative=factual, critical=opinion, emotional=narrative.

📄 Sample Passage with Solutions (CUET Style)

Passage (Factual — Environment Type):
The Amazon rainforest, often called the "lungs of the Earth," produces approximately 20% of the world's oxygen and houses more than 10% of all species on our planet. However, rampant deforestation, largely driven by agricultural expansion and illegal logging, has resulted in the loss of nearly 20% of the Amazon's original cover over the past fifty years. Scientists warn that if deforestation continues at its current rate, the Amazon could reach a tipping point, transforming from a carbon sink into a net carbon emitter. This would accelerate global climate change with catastrophic consequences. Indigenous communities who have lived in harmony with the forest for millennia are the most affected. Conservation efforts, while promising, require strong international cooperation and political will to be truly effective.
Q1: What is the primary cause of Amazon deforestation according to the passage?
A) Climate change
B) Agricultural expansion and illegal logging ✓
C) Natural disasters
D) Industrial pollution
Solution: Passage mein directly likha hai: "driven by agricultural expansion and illegal logging" — Factual question, direct answer.
Q2: The word "rampant" in the passage most closely means:
A) Slow and steady
B) Controlled
C) Widespread and uncontrolled ✓
D) Rare
Solution: Context: "rampant deforestation" — context se pata hai yeh uncontrolled hai. Rampant = widespread, unrestrained.
Q3: The author's tone in this passage is best described as:
A) Optimistic
B) Humorous
C) Concerned and informative ✓
D) Celebratory
Solution: Passage mein "catastrophic consequences", "Scientists warn", "most affected" — ye sab concerned tone show karte hain. Informative facts bhi diye hain. Answer: Concerned + Informative.
Q4: What can be inferred about conservation efforts?
A) They are completely ineffective
B) They are not needed
C) They show promise but need global cooperation ✓
D) They have already solved the problem
Solution: Inference question. Passage says "while promising, require strong international cooperation" → promis ho + cooperation chahiye = Option C.

2️⃣ Articles (a / an / the) HOT

📐 Rules for Articles:
A → consonant sound se start: a book, a university (y-sound), a European
AN → vowel sound (a,e,i,o,u sound): an apple, an hour (h silent), an umbrella
THE → specific/particular noun: the sun, the moon, the Ganga
THE → superlative ke pehle: the best, the tallest
THE → unique things: the President, the Prime Minister
NO ARTICLE → proper nouns: India, Ram, Delhi
NO ARTICLE → abstract nouns generally: Love is blind. (not "The love")
NO ARTICLE → languages: I speak English. (not "the English")
NO ARTICLE → meals: We have dinner at 8. (not "the dinner")
🔥 Tricky Cases:

"An hour" ✓ (h is silent) | "A unique idea" ✓ (unique = yoo-neek, consonant sound)
"The Himalayas" ✓ | "The USA" ✓ | "The Bible" ✓ | "The Times of India" ✓
"He is a/an honest man" → AN ✓ (h silent in honest)

Q: Fill in: He is ___ honour to his profession.
A) a
B) an ✓
C) the
D) no article
Solution: "honour" = 'o' sound (h silent) → AN ✓

3️⃣ Subject-Verb Agreement HAR SAAL

📐 Key Rules:
Rule 1: Singular subject → singular verb: He goes. She writes.
Rule 2: Plural subject → plural verb: They go. Children play.
Rule 3: Two subjects joined by "and" → plural verb: Ram and Sita are happy.
Rule 4: Either/Neither + or/nor → verb agrees with nearest subject:
Either he or they ARE wrong. ✓
Neither she nor I AM going. ✓
Rule 5: Collective nouns (team, jury, family) → singular usually:
The team IS playing. ✓
Rule 6: "Each/Every + noun" → singular: Each student HAS a book. ✓
Rule 7: News/Mathematics/Physics = singular: News IS good. ✓
Rule 8: "A number of" → plural. "The number of" → singular:
A number of students ARE absent. ✓
The number of students IS 30. ✓
Q: Neither the manager nor the employees ___ satisfied with the decision.
A) was
B) were ✓
C) is
D) are
Solution: Neither...nor → verb agrees with nearest subject "employees" (plural) → were ✓
Q: The news ___ shocking to everyone.
A) was ✓
B) were
C) are
D) have been
Solution: "News" always takes singular verb → was ✓

5️⃣ Active–Passive Voice HAR SAAL 2-3 Q

📐 Active → Passive Formula:
Active: Subject + Verb + Object
Passive: Object + be(form) + V₃ + by + Subject

Simple Present: is/am/are + V₃
Present Continuous: is/am/are + being + V₃
Present Perfect: has/have + been + V₃
Simple Past: was/were + V₃
Past Continuous: was/were + being + V₃
Past Perfect: had + been + V₃
Simple Future: will be + V₃
Modals (can/may): can/may + be + V₃
Q: Change to Passive: "She writes a letter."
A) A letter is written by her. ✓
B) A letter was written by her.
C) A letter has been written by her.
D) A letter is being written by her.
Solution: Simple Present → is/am/are + V₃. Object "letter" → subject. She → her. "A letter is written by her." ✓
Q: Change to Passive: "They were building a bridge."
A) A bridge was built by them.
B) A bridge was being built by them. ✓
C) A bridge is being built by them.
D) A bridge had been built by them.
Solution: Past Continuous → was/were + being + V₃. "A bridge was being built by them." ✓
🔥 Pronoun Change in Passive:

I → me | We → us | He → him | She → her | They → them | You → you | It → it
"He hit me" → "I was hit by him." ✓

6️⃣ Direct → Indirect Narration HAR SAAL 2-3 Q

📐 Reporting Verb + Tense Backshift Rules:
Reporting verb = said/told/asked/etc.
Conjunction changes: "that" (statements), "if/whether" (yes-no Q), "wh-word" (wh-Q)

TENSE BACKSHIFT (when reporting verb is past):
Present Simple → Past Simple: "I go" → he said he went
Present Continuous → Past Continuous: "I am going" → he said he was going
Present Perfect → Past Perfect: "I have gone" → he said he had gone
Past Simple → Past Perfect: "I went" → he said he had gone
Will → Would | Can → Could | May → Might | Shall → Should

PRONOUN CHANGES: I→he/she, Me→him/her, We→they, My→his/her
📐 Time & Place Word Changes:
now → then | today → that day | yesterday → the previous day / the day before
tomorrow → the next day / the following day | this → that | these → those
here → there | ago → before | last night → the previous night
Q: Direct: He said, "I am very tired today." → Indirect:
A) He said that he was very tired that day. ✓
B) He said that he is very tired today.
C) He said that he was very tired today.
D) He said that I was very tired that day.
Solution: "am" → "was" (backshift). "today" → "that day". "I" → "he". Answer A ✓
Q: He asked, "Will you help me?" → Indirect:
A) He asked if I will help him.
B) He asked if I would help him. ✓
C) He asked whether I helped him.
D) He asked that I would help him.
Solution: Yes-No question → "if/whether". Will → would. "you" → I (person asking is now reporter). ✓

Para-Jumbles — Sentence Rearrangement
4–5 Questions | HAR SAAL | Moderate Level

📌 Step-by-Step Para-Jumble Solving Method

1
Topic/Introductory Sentence: First sentence usually introduces the main topic broadly. Never starts with "However/But/Therefore/This/That/Such."
2
Pronoun Reference: If a sentence starts with "he/she/it/they/this/that" — find what it refers to in the previous sentence.
3
Connectors/Transitions: "However" = contrast, "Therefore" = result, "Moreover/Furthermore" = addition, "For example" = example (after general statement), "Finally/Lastly" = conclusion.
4
Time/Logical Order: Events follow chronological order. First explain cause, then effect.
5
Conclusion Sentence: Last sentence summarizes/concludes. Often starts with "Thus," "Hence," "In conclusion," "Therefore."
🔥 Golden Rules for Para-Jumbles:

✅ Definite article "THE" → something already mentioned (comes after it's first introduced)
✅ Indefinite "A/AN" → first mention (likely in earlier sentence)
✅ "This/These/Such" → refers to something just said → comes after
✅ "However/But" → introduces contrast → find what it contrasts with
✅ Numbers/lists: "First...Second...Third...Finally" → natural order

Q: Arrange: (P) However, technology also brings problems. (Q) Technology has changed our lives dramatically. (R) For example, social media can cause addiction. (S) In conclusion, we must use technology wisely.
A) PQRS
B) QPRS
C) QPRS ✓
D) SQRP
Solution: Q introduces topic broadly → P contrasts (However) → R gives example of P → S concludes. Order: Q→P→R→S ✓

🔥 Most Common Errors in CUET + How to Spot

Error TypeWrongCorrectRule

B) No error
C) No error
